\section{Introduction}
The pursuit of high-speed, frictionless ground transportation has positioned Electromagnetic Suspension (EMS) systems as a cornerstone of modern Maglev technology.
By utilizing attractive magnetic forces to counteract gravity, EMS systems enable vehicles to "levitate" without physical contact, significantly reducing mechanical wear and energy dissipation.
However, maintaining a stable air gap between the electromagnet and the guideway presents a formidable control challenge.
